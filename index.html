I am a dedicated researcher with a strong foundation in physics, particularly interested in string theory, astro-particle physics, supersymmetry, and general relativity. I hold a BSc from the Lebanese University, where I honed my skills through hands-on experiences in experimental design and data analysis.

My journey in research began with a data analysis internship at the Lebanese American University, where I contributed to a publication on the impact of Lebanon’s economic and energy crises on water security. Following this, I worked as a research assistant at the American University of Beirut, focusing on the development of a diagnostic device for magnetic confinement nuclear fusion devices, which I am currently authoring a paper on.

I aim to deepen my expertise in theoretical particle physics and contribute to advancements in the field through innovative research.

Professional Experience

Research assistant, American University of Beirut (AUB)

Sept, 2023 - Present

Project lead on developing a high-speed stereoscopic diagnostics device in order to detect and identify UFOs, Flakes and Dust particles in the plasma flow of a magnetic confinement nuclear fusion reactor. The objective of this project is to determine particles’ sizes as well as their positions in three dimensions and their velocities in component form. This data plays a pivotal role in informing the necessary adjustments to the input parameters of the device, enabling us to anticipate and mitigate potential disruptions to the plasma flow with greater precision. As I am writing this, we are in the midst of authoring the corresponding paper.

Studying and Using a Prism Spectrometer Goniometer

Optics Laboratory Course, Lebanese University, Faculty of Sciences II

Oct 2023 - Jan 2024

Calibrated the non-digital spectrometer Goniometer using a mercury lamp. The calibrated setup was then used to determine the Balmer spectra of hydrogen lamp and Rydberg constant. A theoretical geometrical optical analysis was performed to determine the best fit of the measured data, the results were plotted using python’s matplotlib library. The concept studied was modeled using Blender.
